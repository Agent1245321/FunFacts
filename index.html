Fun Fact of the Day!

For a period between the 1940s and the 1960s researchers at Berkeley were creating new elements at a rapid rate, attempting to create more elements than the Russian researchers. All of the elements they were creating were highly radioactive and often had short half-lives, also the technology to test those elements weren't at the lab that was set up to actually create the elements. That means individual researchers would have to transport these radioactive elements across long distances themselves. The researchers would create elements at night so they wouldn't have to deal with traffic and then drive as fast as they possibly could, often being chased by the police, to get their research done
